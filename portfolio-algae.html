Detailed Story

The Challenge: Traditional water quality testing involves sending boats to collect samples and analyzing them in labsâ€”a slow and expensive process. My goal was to act as an "optometrist" for lakes, determining water health from a single photo.

Methodology: Initially, we used color extraction (RGB values) to estimate Chlorophyll-a. However, this method was easily fooled by green tree reflections. To solve this, I implemented a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN).

Results: The CNN model successfully distinguishes between harmful algal blooms and harmless reflections by analyzing texture patterns, significantly improving detection accuracy.

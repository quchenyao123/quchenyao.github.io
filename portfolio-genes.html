"Solving the 'Curse of Dimensionality' in genetic data to help patients."

Detailed Story

This project focuses on analyzing high-dimensional gene expression data to classify cancer types accurately.

The Challenge: Gene data is notoriously difficult to handle. It often contains thousands of variables (genes) but very few samples (patients). This "small n, large p" problem makes traditional statistical methods fail.

Solution: I implemented advanced dimensionality reduction techniques (like PCA) and classification algorithms. By filtering out noise and focusing on the most expressive genes, the model can accurately identify cancer subtypes, paving the way for more personalized medical treatments.
